Berliner Döner

Brutalism-inspired web design meets Turkish-German street food culture

Did you know that Berlin has more Kebab shops than Istanbul and houses the biggest Turkish population outside of Turkey? Turkish migrants introduced many new culinary delights to Germany, but one special type of street food transformed Germany: the Döner Kebab.






Process

The challenge was to design a website that not only promotes Berliner Döner's unique food offerings but also resonates with the cultural and architectural vibes of Berlin.

Goals:

  • Create an engaging, visually appealing website.
  • Reflect the essence of Berlin's brutalist architecture.
  • Highlight the unique fusion of Turkish and German culinary traditions.

User Research: Conducted interviews and surveys to understand the target audience's preferences and behaviors.

Market Research: Analyzed competitors and current market trends to identify opportunities and gaps.

Brainstormed and sketched initial ideas. Created mind maps to explore different concepts. Narrowed down to the most promising ideas.

Visual Design

Defined the visual style, color palette, typography, and imagery.

Developed the website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Faced challenges with responsive design and cross-browser compatibility, which were addressed through rigorous testing and iterations.

Testing: Conducted usability testing to gather user feedback and made necessary adjustments.

Outcomes: The project successfully met the goals, receiving positive feedback from users and stakeholders.

Learnings: Gained deeper insights into combining cultural elements with modern web design principles.

Future Improvements: Plan to enhance the website with more interactive features and a mobile app.
