Project Journey

Quick overview from start to finish

Follow a user-centric approach to create a destination microsite that highlights the unique culture and community of a specific location using only HTML and CSS.

Along with extensive secondary research, I conducted primary research through a survey and interviews.

Mood Board

Mood boards are fantastic for collecting inspirational images, color palettes, typefaces, and more. This mood board inspired me to translate Berlin's brutalist architecture into a digital aesthetic, incorporating bright colours to represent multiculturalism and harmony.

Site Map

The site map went through three iterations to determine the most ideal structure of the main page and sub-pages of the microsite. The evolution mainly shows a simplification to reduce the user of having to navigate to too many pages.

Sketches

I developed four concept sketches: three for web and one for mobile. Overall, the sketching process made me realise that I favoured a straight symmetric split layout for balanced content placement as seen in the first web sketch and the fourth mobile sketch.

Berliner D√∂ner came to life through an iterative design process using Figma and translating the final mockups into a prototype using HTML and CSS. This was before ChatGPT so everything was coded from scratch.

Wireframes and Mockups

The first iteration from the sketches involved creating a set of wireframes, mainly centred around the home page. This progressed to finalising all the wireframes for web and mobile and adopting a Brutalist design style. The final stage involved creating mockup designs. Feedback from peers was taken into account throughout this process.
