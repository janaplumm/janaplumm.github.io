About Berliner Neighbourhoods

Kiez is a German word for neighbourhood and is most prominently used in Berlin. The traditional, immigrant Kieze are near the city centre of Berlin and the biggest ethnically diverse districts are found in Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg, Mitte and Neukölln. Many immigrants have a strong connection to their Kiez.


Below are some insider tips on what to do and where to stay in the Kieze!

Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg

What to do

  • Turkish Market

    Tuesdays and Fridays from 11 am to 6.30 pm

  • Jewish Museum

    Contemporary museum designed by famous American architect Daniel Libeskind

  • Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg (FHXB) Museum

    History of migration in Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg

  • Bergmannstraße

    A multicultural street filled with international shops

  • Gayhane at SO36

    On last Saturday of each month, this club hosts an event for queer migrants wanting to listen to familiar music

Where to stay

  • Mövenpick Hotel Berlin

    4-star hotel close to public transport with modern and stylish rooms, gym, sauna and a restaurant

  • Motel One Mitte

    Pet friendly hotel that lets children up to 12 years old stay for free in their parents’ room

  • Wil7 Boutique Hotel

    Boutique hotel featuring classic Berliner architecture and style that offers regional and organic breakfast

  • Exclusive AirBnB Loft

    1 bedroom trendy loft with availability to host 4 guests that comes with a swing and its own gym

Mitte

What to do

  • Pergamonmuseum

    The museum of the ancient Near East and the museum of Islamic Art, perfect to get to know ancient Turkish history and culture

  • Tadshikische Teestube

    Bohemian tearoom with a courtyard offering Russian small plates and vegan specialities

  • Haus Schwarzenberg

    Local history museum, niche shops and street art hotspot

  • Former East Germany (DDR) Museum

    Immerses visitors into the former life of East Germany before the fall of the Wall in 1989

  • Brandenburger Tor

    Important symbol of the unity of Berlin and Germany and an iconic tourist spot

Where to stay

  • Hotel Adlon

    Berlin’s most famous luxury 5 star hotel that even serves the most expensive Döner for 26 EUR, situated right next to the Brandenburg Gate

  • Spacious and family-friendly Apartment

    3 bedroom apartment with a total of 5 beds and capable of hosting up to 8 people with a multilingual host

  • Circus Berlin

    Hybrid hostel and hotel, great for solo travellers on a budget and even has its own microbrewery

  • Boutique Hotel i31

    Elegant boutique hotel that has some rooms placed within old shipping containers on the rooftop and offers electric BMWs free of charge for guests

Neukölln

What to do

  • The Barber Yard

    Visit this traditional and highly recommended Turkish barber shop for all your hair needs

  • Schillermarkt

    A tiny, but integral market of Neukölln that is on every Wednesday and Saturday

  • Klunker Kranich

    Two level rooftop garden that hosts everything from concerts to cinema screenings

  • Sonnenallee

    Visit this famous street in Berlin and watch the iconic German film named after it to learn more about former East Germany

  • Hufeisensiedlung

    If you're an architecture fanatic, go see the UNESCO World Heritage listed housing estate

Where to stay

  • Minimal Hostel

    Highly rated, modern hostel that gives you slippers and bed linen, free wifi and has a shared lounge

  • Stylish Designer Apartment

    A beautiful 19th century, but modern apartment that suits a single traveler or couple with 1 bedroom

  • Hüttenpalast

    Unique and fun accommodation that has retro-style caravans and cabins within two old factory halls

  • Berlin Style Apartments

    Variously sized apartments that are close to public transport and classically furnished
