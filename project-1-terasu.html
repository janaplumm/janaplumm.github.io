To tackle the United Nations (UN) Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 8, our group applied user research, systems thinking and iteration to create a hybrid digital and physical product.






Project Journey

Quick overview from start to finish

In your group, choose one of the 17 United Nations (UN) Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) as your problem space. As part of the user experience/service design stream, you must include a combination of digital, spatial and physical elements in your solution.

After conducting an initial brainstorm, we decided to choose SDG 8, which pertains to promote sustained, inclusive and sustainable economic growth, full productive employment and decent work for all. As final-year university students, we have a personal interest in this matter.

We also found that targets 8.5 and 8.6 are completely off track and in need of a breakthrough according to Monash University.

I took on the responsibility of managing the project in terms of organising the content and duties, and managing our time appropriately. We used FigJam to have a unified, collaborative working space. I created a tabular timeline to track our weekly tasks in anticipation of assignments, which the team appreciated.
