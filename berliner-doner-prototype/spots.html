Top 5

The five best Döner spots curated from food critques, feedback and reviews

1

Rüyam Gemüse Kebab

Tempelhof-Schöneberg

Visit the reigning Kebab King at their original restaurant in Tempelhof-Schöneberg or their newest restaurant in Prenzlauer Berg. They have meat, vegetarian and vegan options.

2

Mustafa’s Gemüse Kebap

Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg

A cult favourite in Berlin since 2006, this food truck is known for its huge line of locals and tourists sometimes waiting for up to 2 hours. Mustafa’s is famous for its Chicken Döner and special secret sauce.

3

Imren Grill

Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg

Imren opened up their first restaurant in 1993 in Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg and has since expanded to a total of six locations. They offer halal products and bake their own flatbreads.

4

NUR Gemüse Kebab

Neukölln

A favourite food truck near the Neukölln public transport stations, NUR is very proud of their Chicken Döner, but also offers a variety of vegetarian options and only uses halal certified products.

5

Vöner

Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg

The first restaurant to offer vegan Döner in Germany. The homemade plant-based meat is made out of wheat protein, vegetables, soy meal, legumes and lots of herbs and spices.

Rising 5

The five Rising Stars based on your recommendations and our checks

1

Döner Turm

Mitte

This late-night snack bar is the first thing you smell as you exit the nearby subway station and is known for its friendly service. You can enjoy your Döner standing or take a seat on one of the outdoor benches.

2

Hakiki Döner

Mitte

Get ready to wait a bit in line for this Döner, because everyone wants to get a taste of the secret homemade sauce they add. Hakkiki might be a chain, but you can expect excellent quality at any spot.

3

Kebap With Attitude

Mitte

This contemporary restaurant has a cool vibe and is living up to Berlin’s trendy scene by adding vegan cauliflower options and craftbeer to the menu. If you’re a fan, you can buy their stylish merch.

4

Rosenthaler Grill

Mitte

Offering a diverse culinary experience with a focus on Italian and Turkish dishes, the Rosenthaler Grill in Mitte offers a casual atmosphere where you can find something for everyone on the menu.

5

Hisar Fresh Food

Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg

This place is one of the oldest in Berlin having opened up in 1989 after the fall of the Berlin Wall. Here you can also try Turkish classics like Lahmacun (Turkish Pizza) and Dürüm (Turkish Wrap).
