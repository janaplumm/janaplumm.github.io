Connecting career starters with industry mentors to build strong soft skills

In 2024, Australia's youth unemployment rate sits at 9.5% compared with the general unemployment rate of 4.1%. To tackle the United Nations (UN) Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 8, our group applied user research, systems thinking and iteration to create a hybrid digital and physical solution.






Project Journey

Quick overview from start to finish

In your group, choose one of the 17 United Nations (UN) Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) as your problem space. As part of the user experience/service design stream, you must include a combination of digital, spatial and physical elements in your solution.

After conducting an initial brainstorm, we decided to choose SDG 8, which pertains to promote sustained, inclusive and sustainable economic growth, full productive employment and decent work for all. As final-year university students, we have a personal interest in this matter.

We also found that targets 8.5 and 8.6 are completely off track and in need of a breakthrough according to Monash University (2020).

Targets 8.5 and 8.6:
  • 8.5: By 2030, achieve full and productive employment and decent work for all women and men, including for young people and persons with disabilities, and equal pay for work of equal value.
  • 8.6: By 2020, substantially reduce the proportion of youth not in employment, education or training.

Therefore, we decided to focus on career starters to account for youth, school leavers and people pivoting their careers.

I took on the responsibility of managing the project in terms of organising the content and duties, and managing our time appropriately. We used FigJam to have a unified, collaborative working space. I created a tabular timeline to track our weekly tasks in anticipation of assignments.

Research was conducted using both secondary and primary methods. The desktop research began as a diverging process by delving into academic papers, statistics and news articles, and later converged by narrowing the focus for user research.

Overview of Desktop Research
  • Investigated the current state of the Australian labour market, finding an increase in youth unemployment from 2023 to 2024
  • Discovered that 75% of Australian employers value soft skills as much as, or more than, technical skills
  • Identified three significant issues through our desktop research: Skill development, access to information and technology and recruitment challenges
  • Tools like the competitor analysis and stakeholder mapping helped us better understand the larger ecosystem

Overview of User Research
  • Formulated four hypotheses focusing on the 'how' or 'why' based on our desktop research findings
  • Conducted a survey (36 responses), 16 interviews and an online ethnography targeting social media communities
  • Employed research triangulation to gather data from anecdotal, cultural, and personal user insights
  • Derived four insights through analysing our findings with an affinity diagram
