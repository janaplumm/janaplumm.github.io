About Berliner Döner

Berlin is home to the biggest Turkish population outside of Turkey. Turkish migrants introduced many new flavours to Germany, but one type of food became a hybrid between the two cultures and that is the Döner Kebab. The Döner Kebab is the pride of Berlin and a symbol of positive multiculturalism.


Below are some facts about Berlin, the Döner and Turkey!

Berlin

  • Founded in the 13th century, Berlin has undergone many radical changes from Imperalisim to the National Socialist era to the fall of the Wall in 1989

  • 3.7 million people live in Berlin

  • Berlin is the most multicultural city in Germany with people from around 190 countries and 106.000 Turkish citizens

  • Has more Döner spots than Istanbul that together sell 400.000 Döners a day

  • Has more museums (175) than rainy days (99)

  • The Berlinale, Berlin’s famous international film festival, attracts thousands of film lovers and stars every year

  • Has over 250 weekly markets spread across the city

Döner

  • The Döner is a German-Turkish hybrid food based on the Turkish kebab with German influences like salad, vegetables and lots of sauces

  • Turkish Gastarbeiter were invited to Germany from the 1960s onwards to help rebuild the economy after the Second World War

  • Kadir Nurman, a guest worker from Turkey, is said to have invented the Döner in 1972 in West Berlin

  • Nurman transformed the Döner from a plate-based dish to a takeaway meal that busy Berliners could enjoy on the go

  • 2.5 million Döners are eaten on a daily basis in Germany

  • Elon Musk famously tweeted that the Döner is his favourite German food

Turkey

  • Turkey is home to one of the earliest human settlements in the world

  • Major historical events and people influenced Turkey like the Trojan War, Alexander the Great, the Byzantine and Ottoman Empires

  • Population of 81 million people

  • Ankara is the capital city

  • Geographically bridges the continents of Europe and Asia and is surrounded by the Black Sea, the Mediterranean Sea and the Aegean Sea

  • The culture is very family orientated and hospitable to visitors

  • Diet primarily features lamb, eggplant and yoghurt
